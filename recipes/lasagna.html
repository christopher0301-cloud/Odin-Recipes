Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a hearty Italian pasta casserole made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich tomato or meat ragù, creamy béchamel or ricotta, and melty cheeses like mozzarella and Parmesan, then baking until bubbly and golden. The dish is known for its soft yet structured layers, where tender noodles soak up savory sauces and melted cheese to create a comforting, oven-baked meal often served in generous, square portions.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Tomato sauce
  • Béchamel sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Heat the ground beef in a large skillet over medium heat until browned.
  4. Add the tomato sauce and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Prepare the béchamel sauce in a separate pan.
  6. Assemble the lasagna by layering the noodles, meat sauce, béchamel sauce, and ricotta cheese in a baking dish.
  7. Sprinkle mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses on top.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden and bubbly.
